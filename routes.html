Recommended Walking Routes

Route 1 – Terézváros Architecture Walk (District VI)

This route explores the quieter residential streets of Terézváros, an area known for its elegant late-19th-century apartment buildings and ornate facades. Walking along streets such as Eötvös utca, Szondi utca, and Aradi utca, visitors can observe beautiful balconies, decorative entrances, and historic courtyards. The area also contains several small cafés and local bakeries that make it a pleasant place to take a break during a walk.

Route 2 – Diplomatic Quarter Garden Walk (District VI–XIV)

This route leads through the peaceful Diplomatic Quarter, where many embassies and historic villas are located. Streets such as Benczúr utca and Délibáb utca are lined with trees and impressive buildings from the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The area has a calm atmosphere and wide sidewalks, making it ideal for a relaxing walk away from busy tourist areas.

Route 3 – Inner Erzsébetváros Courtyard Walk (District VII)

This walk focuses on the historic inner courtyards and unique architectural details of Erzsébetváros. Many buildings in this district hide beautiful inner courtyards behind their street facades. Walking along Kazinczy utca, Dob utca, and nearby streets, visitors can discover hidden passages, colorful street art, and historic apartment houses that reflect the diverse cultural history of the area.
